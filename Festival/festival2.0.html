The Festival

Where the Caribbean
reinvents itself

CARIBE VIVO is a one-of-a-kind gathering where avant-garde cuisine meets the biodiversity of the Colombian Caribbean. A space to celebrate ancestral ingredients, contemporary techniques, and the creative spirit of our region.

Four days of immersive experiences designed for those who understand gastronomy as cultural expression: workshops, tastings, conversations, and chef's dinners that redefine what it means to eat in the tropics.

Protagonists

The festival's
chefs

Guest Chef · Barranquilla

Valentina Ospino

Pioneer in reinterpreting San Andrés cuisine through fermentation techniques and the use of native plants from the archipelago.

Head Chef · Cartagena

Marcos de la Hoz

Renowned for his work with Pacific and Caribbean coastal ingredients, translating biodiversity into unique sensory experiences.

Guest Chef · Santa Marta

Isabela Cure

Specialist in ancestral Wayuu cuisine, researcher of forgotten crops, and advocate for gastronomy as cultural memory.
